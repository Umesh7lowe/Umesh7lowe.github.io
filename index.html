About

I am a PhD researcher in the School of Civil Engineering at University College Dublin, specialising in transportation management and planning. My work sits at the intersection of behavioural science and transport policy — asking not just how people travel, but why, and how we can design systems that nudge them toward smarter, greener choices.

My PhD project is part of NexSys, a national research programme advancing next-generation energy systems in Ireland. The project explores sustainable mobility and its role in the broader energy transition — connecting transport behaviour with Ireland's clean energy future.

My academic journey spans three countries. I began with a BSc in Civil Engineering at the University of Peradeniya in Sri Lanka, went on to earn a First Class MSc in Transportation & Mobility Management from Thammasat University in Thailand, and am now completing my PhD at UCD — one of Europe's leading research institutions.

Beyond research, I have spent years in the classroom as a Teaching Assistant and Instructor, guiding students through complex methodologies and fostering the next generation of engineers. I bring the same rigour and curiosity to every collaboration — whether in the field, the lab, or the lecture hall.

Outside academia, you'll find me on a badminton court, playing volleyball, or kayaking rivers to clean them. I also volunteer as a STEAM Instructor with Simply Science, helping inspire the next generation of young scientists and engineers across Ireland.
